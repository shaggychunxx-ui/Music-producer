Note FX Input Mode
Found in the Inspector (F4), Note FX Input Mode controls whether Note Events are recorded before or after they are passed through the
Note FX plug-ins on a given Instrument Track. This allows for rendering of processed Note Events to an Instrument Track while recording
through Note FX. The default setting is off.
On: incoming note events are recorded to the Instrument Track before they pass through the Note FX plug-in
Off: incoming note events are processed first, and then the output of the Note FX plug-in is recorded to the Instrument Track.
An example: turning Note FX Input Mode On would allow you to record Note Events as processed by the Arpeggiator — and allow you to
edit each individual note by hand after recording.
Collaboration
What you can do with Studio One+ Collaboration Tools
Collaboration tools allow Studio One users to work collaboratively with other users on Songs, Projects, and Shows (collectively referred
to as “documents”) via online file sharing in MyPreSonus or Studio One+ .
With Studio One’s Collaboration tools, there’s no need to zip up your work and upload it to Studio One+ or a cloud drive from a web
browser — the tools are built right into Studio One.
Some ideas for online collaboration include:
-
Send a Song full of instrumental tracks to a singer for them to record their vocal tracks and send the Song back to you
-
Send a mastering Project full of mixed songs to a mastering engineer who owns plug-ins and hardware you may not have
access to
-
Send a Show to your worship team leader to collaborate on the the Setlist and backing tracks for the next performance
While these are perhaps the most obvious use cases for Collaboration, we’re sure you will come up with new and exciting ways to col-
laborate that we never even dreamed of!
Who can use Studio One+ Collaboration Tools
At least one member in a group of collaborators must have a Studio One+ membership. Once a Studio One+ member has created a
shared Workspace, anyone with a MyPreSonus account can be invited to collaborate.

Notes on Collaboration
Collaborating with yourself
If you have multiple workstations (perhaps a desktop and a laptop computer), you can share a document from one of them to one of your
own Workspaces and join the same document on the other computer to start working on the document on both computers. If you do not
invite collaborators to this workspace, you can privately share the document on all of your computers.
You can also use the “Send” feature with a private Workspace to maintain an easy cloud backup of your workfiles.
Invitations and Workspaces
Note that invitations to collaborators operate on the Workspace level, and document sharing is handled on the document level. It’s not
possible to invite someone to a Workspace without granting them access to every document folder in that Workspace.
Why are some folders dimmed out?
Workspace folders can be dimmed out (inaccessible) for a couple of different reasons.
Collaborating with Others

-
In the Join menu, Workspace folders will be dimmed out if they do not contain a shared document.
-
In the Share menu, Workspace folders will be dimmed out when they already contain a shared document.
Why can’t I see the Workspace I was invited to?
Workspaces may not display in the Collaboration menus for a couple of different reasons.
-
You need to accept the invitation in your MyPreSonus/Studio One+ account before the Workspace is visible in the Col-
laboration menus. If the Collaboration menu was open when you accepted the invitation, click the “Refresh” button.
-
Your Workspace permission may be set to “Browse.” A Workspace with your permission set to Browse will not be shown
when you try to Share one of your documents. You will also be unable to Send updates if your permission is set to Browse.
Sending and Receiving Documents
Exchanging updated documents is the core function of Collaboration. Both Sending and Receiving of document updates are managed
via the File >> Collaboration menu. After making any changes to your document, (recording new tracks; updating a mix; tweaking Auto-
mation, etc.) you and your collaborators can share the updated work with one another from the File >> Collaboration menu.
A Folder shared via Collaboration can only be shared to a single Workspace at a time.
Sending your document updates to your collaborators
Once you’ve made some changes to a shared document, you can upload these changes to Studio One+ for your collaborators.
To Send updates of your document to your collaborators, Use File >> Collaboration >>Send… to upload your changes to your shared
Workspace. Your Collaborators will be notified of the update in their Notification Center.
Receiving document updates from your collaborators
If one of your collaborators has made a change to a shared document and committed their changes by Sending them to Studio One+ ,
you can download these updated files to your local workstation.
To receive edits to your document made by collaborators, use File >> Collaboration >> Receive to download their document changes to
your local workstation. Alternatively, you can download these changes by clicking Receive on the appropriate Notification in the Noti-
fication Center.
Sending and Receiving Documents

Canceling a Send or Receive
During a Send or Receive operation, you’ll be presented with the Transfers Window. Right-click any transfer in progress to receive an
option to cancel the Transfer. This will abort the transfer and update of all files, even if the Transfer operation is nearly complete. This
avoids inconsistent states in the workspace or your local folder.
Temporary Document Locking
During a Send or Receive operation, the Studio One+ Folder containing the shared document will be locked, preventing further changes
from other collaborators. This prevents errors that could occur from multiple users sending or receiving changes at the same time. The
lock will be cleared when the Send or Receive operation has concluded.
documents cannot be saved during a Send/Receive operation.
Unlinking Shared Documents
If you would like to exit a Collaboration, choose File >> Collaboration >> Unlink.
Unlinking removes the local link to the shared document. The formerly shared content in Studio One+ will remain there.
You will no longer be able to Send updates to it.
You will no longer be able to Receive updates from other collaborators.
Existing collaborators can still Send and Receive updates to each other.
You can rejoin a Collaboration at any time using File >>Collaboration >> Join, assuming you still have the required permissions.
To end a Collaboration completely, delete the Workspace from within your Studio One+ account. This will not delete the local files on col-
laborator workstations.
Notes on file sharing in Collaborations
Which files are shared in a Collaboration
When Sharing a document from Studio One’s Song, Show, or Project page, the document’s Folder and files therein (excluding Studio
One cached and temporary files) are shared to the selected Studio One+ Workspace.
Only files that are newly-created or modified since the last Send/Receive operation will be uploaded to Studio One+ in a Send operation.
Un-changed files are not shared. This cuts down on file size and transfer speed.
Files shared include:
The root shared folder, its files, and its subfolders
.Song, .Project, and .Show Files
Any subfolders and their content that you have added to the shared document folder. This is a great way to send album art, samples, lyr-
ics documents, or anything else you’d like.
Which files are not shared
In a Send or Receive operation, not all files are shared. This is a significant advantage over cloud drive services, where sharing non-
critical files would increase transfer times and use up disk space unnecessarily.
Sending and Receiving Documents

Autosave, Temp, and Cache files are not shared via Collaboration tools.
You cannot share a file over 5GB in size. Video files can be quite large; consider using low-resolution versions of your video files when
collaborating on film and video projects.
Removing unused files
If you have removed any assets from your document before Sending, you can choose “Remove Unused Files” from the dialogue box dur-
ing a share operation. Click the “Delete Files Permanently” option to delete the unused files from your local workstation and exclude them
from the Send operation to reduce the transfer size. Be sure these files are not used by other/older versions of the document, or doc-
ument versions of your collaborators.
Overwrite protection
It’s possible that you and a collaborator could make different changes to local copies of the same file around the same time. Col-
laboration Tools have some systems in place to prevent conflicting file versions or accidental overwrites.
For example, let’s say you make a local change to a Shared file, (for example, a .song file) around the same time that your collaborator
does, and your collaborator Sent the Song to the Workspace — but you have local changes to your version of the Song that are not yet
Sent to the Workspace.
When you run a Receive operation, your collaborator’s version of the file will not overwrite your work. Instead, your file will be re-named
during the Receive operation and saved alongside the newly-received version.
Additionally, If you attempt to re-share a document to its original shared Folder after an Unlinking operation, you won’t be able to save to
the original Folder. A new Folder name will be suggested instead.
Sharing from non-default locations
Be wary of sharing documents from locations like “Desktop” or “Downloads.” If you attempt to do this, Studio One will present a con-
firmation dialogue. Sharing from these locations will result in the entire contents of your Desktop or Downloads folder being shared with
your Collaborators.
This could be potentially embarrassing depending on your download history.

Notifications
Available from your User Profile icon at the top right of Studio One, the Notification Center keeps you informed of changes to your Col-
laboration Workspaces. Here, you’ll be notified of updates to your Studio One+ Collaborations, including collaboration invites, new/de-

You’ll also be notified of updates from the Studio One+ Community, including post replies, comments, likes, and mentions.

Deleting Workspaces
Workspaces can be deleted from your Studio One+ account. Click “Settings” on the top right of the Workspace page, and choose “Delete
Workspace” from the pop-up menu
Deleting a Workspace will remove the Workspace and all Folders from Studio One+ . This will not remove the linked files on Collaborator
workstations. If you attempt to Send a document to a deleted Workspace, you will receive an option to Unlink your document.
